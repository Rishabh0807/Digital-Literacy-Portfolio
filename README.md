Task 3 – Social Media Checklist for College Students
✅ Do's

Think before you post or share any content.
Maintain a respectful and positive tone online.
Protect your privacy by limiting personal information.
Verify information before sharing to avoid spreading misinformation.
Use social media for learning, networking, and personal growth.

❌ Don'ts

Don't post offensive, abusive, or inappropriate content.
Don't share fake news or unverified information.
Don't engage in cyberbullying or online arguments.
Don't overshare personal details like phone number, address, or passwords.
Don't let social media negatively affect your studies or mental health.

Task 5 – Cybercrime Awareness Resource
Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud
A detailed cybercrime awareness document covering:

How UPI fraud works step-by-step (targeting → trust → pretext → collect request → funds transferred → disappearance)
Who is most at risk (students, first-time users, OLX sellers, elderly individuals)
Consequences: immediate financial loss, psychological distress, limited legal recourse

Cybercrime Prevention Checklist highlights include:

Use strong, unique passwords (12+ characters) with a password manager
Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) everywhere
Never share OTPs or PINs — no bank will ever ask for them
UPI Safety: Entering your PIN always debits money — never enter a PIN to "receive" a payment
Avoid public Wi-Fi for banking; use mobile data or a VPN
Review app permissions regularly

Report cybercrime at: cybercrime.gov.in | National Helpline: 1930
